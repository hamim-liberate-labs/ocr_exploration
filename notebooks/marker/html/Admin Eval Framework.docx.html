Union City Administrative Effectiveness Framework

(Modeled after Danielson, tailored for non-instructional administrators)

Framework Overview

This framework mirrors Danielson's four-domain structure (Planning/Preparation, Classroom Environment, Instruction, Professionalism) but is adapted for non-instructional administrators such as the Maintenance Supervisor, Bus/Transportation Supervisor, Athletic Director, and Food Service Director.

Domains:

    1. Leadership & Vision Strategic planning, alignment to mission, resource management.
    1. Operations & Management Safety, efficiency, compliance, crisis response.
    1. Collaboration & Communication Stakeholder engagement, team leadership, conflict resolution.
    1. Professionalism & Growth Ethical practice, continuous learning, contribution to district culture.

Each domain includes four levels of performance:

  • Unsatisfactory
  • Basic
  • Proficient
  • Distinguished

Maintenance Supervisor

Domain 1: Leadership & Vision

  • Unsatisfactory: Lacks a facilities plan; responds only when problems arise.
  • Basic: Maintains a basic schedule of repairs and communicates some goals.
  • Proficient: Develops and follows a preventative maintenance plan aligned to district goals.
  • Distinguished: Anticipates future facility needs, secures resources, and models innovation in maintenance practices.

Domain 2: Operations & Management

  • Unsatisfactory: Safety violations or frequent breakdowns occur.
  • Basic: Addresses issues as they arise, but preventive planning is limited.
  • Proficient: Consistently maintains safe, efficient facilities with compliance to codes.
  • Distinguished: Creates systems to maximize efficiency, sustainability, and safety across all facilities.

Domain 3: Collaboration & Communication

  • Unsatisfactory: Rarely communicates with staff or administrators.
  • Basic: Shares updates only when asked.
  • Proficient: Regularly updates principals/administrators and responds promptly to concerns.

  • Distinguished: Builds trust, communicates proactively, and involves stakeholders in planning.

  • Unsatisfactory: Does not pursue new practices or demonstrate professionalism.

  • Basic: Complies with minimum requirements.
  • Proficient: Engages in professional learning, models ethical practice.
  • Distinguished: Leads district initiatives (sustainability, safety drills, training others).

Bus/Transportation Supervisor

Domain 1: Leadership & Vision

  • Unsatisfactory: Lacks organized bus routes; little alignment with district goals.
  • Basic: Routes and schedules are functional but inefficient.
  • Proficient: Routes are efficient, safe, and support instructional priorities.
  • Distinguished: Innovates in transportation (technology for tracking, routing efficiency) and plans proactively.

Domain 2: Operations & Management

  • Unsatisfactory: Frequent late buses, poor vehicle maintenance, safety lapses.
  • Basic: Meets minimum compliance with safety inspections.
  • Proficient: Ensures reliable routes, maintains vehicles, complies with safety and state guidelines.
  • Distinguished: Implements systems for continuous improvement in safety, efficiency, and service quality.

Domain 3: Collaboration & Communication

  • Unsatisfactory: Limited communication with parents or schools.
  • Basic: Communicates reactively when problems arise.
  • Proficient: Maintains clear communication with drivers, principals, and families.

  • Distinguished: Builds strong partnerships with families, staff, and community; anticipates concerns.

  • Unsatisfactory: Does not enforce safety or professional standards with staff.

  • Basic: Provides occasional training or reminders to drivers.
  • Proficient: Regularly trains drivers and models professionalism.
  • Distinguished: Leads regional/state transportation initiatives and mentors peers.

Athletic Director

Domain 1: Leadership & Vision

  • Unsatisfactory: No clear vision; athletic program lacks direction.
  • Basic: Provides scheduling and oversight but limited long-term planning.
  • Proficient: Develops programs aligned with district mission, ensuring opportunities for all students.
  • Distinguished: Champions student-athlete growth, equity, and excellence; anticipates trends and innovates.

Domain 2: Operations & Management

  • Unsatisfactory: Frequent scheduling conflicts, noncompliance with MHSAA rules, or safety issues.
  • Basic: Meets compliance but with some errors or inefficiencies.
  • Proficient: Ensures smooth operations, compliance, and safe facilities.
  • Distinguished: Builds efficient systems, maximizes participation, and ensures high-quality experiences for athletes and families.

Domain 3: Collaboration & Communication

  • Unsatisfactory: Rare communication with coaches, parents, or community.
  • Basic: Communicates only essential information reactively.
  • Proficient: Maintains strong relationships with coaches, parents, and administrators.

  • Distinguished: Builds community partnerships, promotes athletics as an integral part of district culture.

  • Unsatisfactory: Displays bias or unprofessional behavior.

  • Basic: Meets compliance but does not model professional growth.
  • Proficient: Models fairness, seeks professional learning, enforces standards.
  • Distinguished: Mentors coaches, engages in regional/state leadership, promotes holistic student-athlete development.

Food Service Director

Domain 1: Leadership & Vision

  • Unsatisfactory: Program lacks clear goals; poor alignment with student nutrition needs.
  • Basic: Meets federal/state requirements but lacks vision.
  • Proficient: Plans nutritious menus aligned with district goals and student well-being.
  • Distinguished: Innovates menus, integrates farm-to-school or wellness initiatives, anticipates future needs.

Domain 2: Operations & Management

  • Unsatisfactory: Health code violations, frequent shortages, or inefficiencies.
  • Basic: Meets minimum compliance with some lapses.
  • Proficient: Ensures safe, efficient, and cost-effective operations.
  • Distinguished: Implements innovative systems for sustainability, cost savings, and improved service.

Domain 3: Collaboration & Communication

  • Unsatisfactory: Poor communication with staff, students, or families.
  • Basic: Shares limited updates or responds only when asked.
  • Proficient: Maintains effective communication with stakeholders and staff.

  • Distinguished: Engages families, promotes nutrition education, builds positive community partnerships.

  • Unsatisfactory: Lacks professional standards; does not seek improvement.

  • Basic: Meets compliance with limited growth.
  • Proficient: Seeks professional learning, models ethical practice, supports staff.
  • Distinguished: Leads wellness initiatives, mentors peers, and contributes to districtwide student success goals.

Technology Director

Domain 1: Leadership & Vision

  • Unsatisfactory: No technology plan; reacts to issues.
  • Basic: Maintains systems with limited planning.
  • Proficient: Develops and implements a district technology plan aligned to goals.
  • Distinguished: Anticipates future needs, drives innovation, aligns vision with instructional priorities.

Domain 2: Operations & Management

  • Unsatisfactory: Frequent outages, poor data security.
  • Basic: Resolves issues reactively; compliance inconsistent.
  • Proficient: Maintains reliable infrastructure, ensures compliance, protects data..
  • Distinguished: Implements proactive systems, maximizes efficiency, ensures robust cybersecurity.

Domain 3: Collaboration & Communication

  • Unsatisfactory: Rarely communicates with staff/admin..
  • Basic: Provides updates only when asked.
  • Proficient: Communicates effectively with staff, leaders, and vendors.

  • Distinguished: Proactively engages stakeholders, builds trust, and empowers staff with training.

  • Unsatisfactory: Avoids learning new tech, lacks professionalism.

  • Basic: Meets compliance with limited growth.
  • Proficient: Seeks professional learning, models ethics, supports staff.
  • Distinguished: Leads district innovation, mentors peers, contributes to regional/state initiatives.).

Business Manager

Domain 1: Leadership & Management

  • Unsatisfactory: Fails to provide oversight of district finances; budgets are inaccurate or incomplete; does not comply with legal requirements.
  • Basic: Maintains budgets and financial processes but with occasional errors; limited communication with stakeholders.
  • Proficient: Consistently manages budgets accurately, ensures compliance, and communicates financial information clearly to stakeholders.
  • Distinguished: Anticipates financial needs, proactively guides district leaders, and uses financial data to strategically support district goals.

Domain 2: Fiscal Responsibility & Compliance

  • Unsatisfactory: Frequently late or inaccurate with state/federal reporting; financial audits reveal serious deficiencies.
  • Basic: Completes reports and audits with occasional corrections needed; limited long-term financial planning.
  • Proficient: Reports are accurate, timely, and audits are clean; engages in sound financial forecasting.
  • Distinguished: Provides exemplary transparency, identifies cost-saving opportunities, and secures additional revenue streams (e.g., grants, partnerships).

Domain 3: Communication & Collaboration

  • Unsatisfactory: Withholds or provides unclear financial information; limited communication with administrators or board.
  • Basic: Shares required information but without context or clarity; collaboration is limited.
  • Proficient: Provides clear, understandable financial information to administrators, board, and community; works collaboratively on budget priorities.
  • Distinguished: Proactively engages stakeholders, builds financial literacy in the district, and creates a culture of trust and transparency.

Domain 4: Professionalism & Ethical Standards

  • Unsatisfactory: Engages in unethical practices or conflicts of interest; disregards district policy.
  • Basic: Adheres to policies but may lack transparency or initiative.
  • Proficient: Demonstrates integrity, confidentiality, and adherence to policies and laws.
  • Distinguished: Models the highest ethical standards, mentors others in ethical practice, and safeguards district resources with exceptional stewardship.
