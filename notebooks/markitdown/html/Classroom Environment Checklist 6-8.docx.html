Clean Classrooms
------------------ --- --- ---
● No clutter (e.g. empty boxes, random papers, broken
computers)
● Windowsills cleared off (e.g. no loose papers)
● Shelving units are kept tidy
● Anchor Charts are visible to students and current
● Posters are not torn, wrinkled, or old
●
Counters and tables are kept clean and organized (e.g. no
pencil shavings, dust, student belongings, etc.)
● Student desks are kept clean and empty
●
Teacher desk is organized and clean
Signage
--- --- --- ---
All areas of the classroom must be clearly labeled (e.g.. classroom
library, portfolios, bulletin boards, notebooks, bins, binders). When
possible signs should be electronically printed. If they are
handwritten, they must be legible, attractive and neat.
Instructional Charts/Anchor Charts
------------- ------------- ------ ---
Instructional charts are vital for displaying what you have been
teaching. Students use these as a resource, and they bring the units
alive in your classroom. All charts should be current and relevant,
hanging straight, and not torn or crumpled.
See bottom of checklist for examples
Portfolio Assessment Folders
--------- ------------------ --- ---
Designated folder which includes:
● Table of Contents for Performance Tasks (indicates if piece is
digital or on paper)

● Table of Contents for Unit Assessment (e.g. tests, quizzes)

All items in this folder must include rubrics and feedback. Bulletin Boards (Classroom and Hallway) All backing paper must be replaced for the 2024-2025 school year. Designated sections for each content area, clearly labeled, with authentic student work of varying levels (at least 10 student samples) and meaningful feedback based on a rubric. Feedback should include a specific next step and a strategy for getting there, as well as one area of celebration, both based on the rubric. Bulletin Boards should include: Teacher name(s), Date, Standards addressed, Task, Rubric, Student work with feedback. Classroom Library/Resource Center (labeled as such) All bins/books should be easily accessible to students, and bins should be maintained and kept tidy. All ELA/ENL rooms must have a clearly labeled library with books organized by genre. All other contents should have content specific libraries/resource centers. Safety ● Visible and accessible Red Emergency Folder (includes Assembly Cards) and updated student rosters ● Allergy notification system (ie. Nut Free Zone sign provided by Admin) as needed Additional Classroom Expectations: ● Food and Drinks: No glass bottles are permitted in the school building. If you permit students to eat in the classroom, ensure that all trash is properly removed. ● Outer Clothing: Make sure students have removed their hoods, caps, hats and jackets when they are in your room. If students are cold, they are permitted to wear long sleeves under their uniform shirts. ● School Bags: Make sure that all school bags have been removed and placed on the back of students’ chairs. ● Sports Equipment: Sports equipment is not allowed to be visible in the classroom or hallways during instructional time. If sports equipment becomes a distraction to teaching and learning it must be taken from the student.

● Cell Phones: Phones are collected in the morning and returned at the end of the school day.
